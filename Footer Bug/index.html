Kids . In . Afrika

Zolani Mali is a hip hop artist and producer based in Durban and is a member of the hip hop collective Kids In Afrika. He was born in Queenstown in the Eastern Cape and moved to Welkom in the Free State at the age of nine.

He was introduced to hip-hop by his Uncle, who used to listen to westcoast acts such as Tupac Shakur and Bone Thugs N Harmony. He later fell in-love with the genre himself listening to Eminem and was inspired to write his first rap. In high school he joined a rap group where he found out about the underground hip hop scene both locally and abroad and was heavily influenced by acts such as Hymphatic Thabs, Tumi and the Volume, Max Normal locally as well as Aesop Rock, Atmosphere, El-P, Mos Def and MF DOOM internationally.

He gravitated towards acts which had a distinctive cadence coupled with vivid imagery in their writing and this is evidenced by elements in his own writing style. His work aims to hold up the mirror to society and reflect all the things the society goes through, the hurt, pain, sadness and happiness, love and hate. He's fascinated by duality particularly the cooperation of contradicting facets and views himself as a misfit being Xhosa and raised in a Sotho environment, being black while being in an Afrikaans school.

He's collaborated with acts such as Supah Mpondo, Mabhiyoza, Elohymn, This Guy That Guy, MikeArch and Mary Street but to name a few in the hip hop scene in Durban.

He's currently doing his final year at the University of KwaZulu Natal where he met the members of Kids In Afrika. He's known by the monikers Pendragon and Zol_animal but prefers to just be called by his given name, Zolani.

Somdaka, Lakhe born 25 July 1996 is from the Eastern Cape and is currently based in the city of Durban, KZN. He is a lover of words and is passionate about music and art as a whole. He pays special attention to detail and is very expressive in a witty way. The balance between poet and rapper makes him so versatile that the name “This Guy That Guy” is perfectly fitting. He has this ability to take up any character and bring it to life, this being one of the reasons he went for this name and calls himself “The Schizophrenic Prince of Rap”, he believes he has multiple personalities that need to be expressed through the art. He started writing when he was in grade 6 with the help of his English teacher who would assist him to prepare speeches for public speaking competitions, debate and reading competitions. He developed the love for poetry at the age of 15 when he realized that he can pour his heart out about personal issues that bothered him and still be discreet, he calls it “hiding in plain sight”. Although the love for music is a thing he had since a young age as he’s Mother used to play Izitibili (Choral music), he developed the interest to rap and make music when he was in grade 8 after witnessing his very first rap battle and started listening to underground acts, he has since then never looked back. He later became a member of an art hub in the University of Kwa-Zulu Natal, Westville Campus, called Keen Artist Theatre, where he groomed himself as he grew and became on the founding members of the musical movement Kids in Afrika (KIA).

Mpumelelo fihlela, also known as Elohymn(stage name) is a rapper, singer, song writer guitarist and beat maker from KZN at Empangeni township. He started music back in 2010 at age 15 with a group of other rappers called Rap mobsters, then through out his journey things fell of with the group and went to discover a new membership with another group of artists and they start a movement called kids in Afrika.
The aim of his music is to empower black kids in Afrika with the knowledge of self and love, it aims to bring emotional bonds in the hearts of black brothers and sisters as he believes that black on black battles whether psychic or physical, root from this lack of emotional bonds.

Siyamthemba Dlamini, commonly referred to as his stage alias Mary Street- born on the 14
February 1998 hails from the dusty roads of Umzimkhulu. Having grown up in an area rife with
substance abuse and destitution, music became a refuge leading up to him joining a youth church  choir. Where he discovered a love for writing and instruments. His passion for music transpired
into his adult life when he arrived in Durban as the universe conspired to surround him with like
minded friends. Through these friendships emerged the music group “Kids in Africa” aka
“KIA”. Mary Street is a producer, song writer and rapper. He is currently doing his penultimate
year at the University of KwaZulu Natal enrolled for a BSC. He has worked with renowned
Durban artists Supah Mpondo, award winning producer Neo Ndawo, Scorch Supernova, and
other up and coming artists such as St. Cloud, L.B nefelibata and Harmony sound records. His
sound is influenced by a home that always had music close their hearts. Whether it was singing
hymns at church or listening to soul brothers on Sunday afternoons, these greatly contributed to his understanding and feel in music. His love for hip hop and rap developed at a tender age,
always being around older siblings who introduced him to legends like 2 pac, the notorious
B.I.G, Bone thugs and Harmony, Jay-Z, Nas, Dr dre, Eminem and many more others that he
would go on to look up to in his early childhood. Mary Street’s ambitions include breaking
through the Durban music industry, the national and eventually the international music scene, he is definitely someone to look out for. You can come along on the journey by following him on the following social media platforms:

Rapper/Producer

MikeArch (The Young Pharoah) born Moeka Michael Molise II found his humble beginnings in a single parent household in the small town of Kroonstad, finding Solace in expressive art. In the early stages of his life his first oulet was in the form of grafitti and comic book sketches coupled with his appreciation for Rap music and 90's RnB, these became the stepping stones to writting his own music.
In his teen years he found a love for dance and engaged with several youth programmes LoveLife being one of them. It's during these times he began sharpening his skills in ciphers and collabs with local artists. He enrolled at UKZN in 2011 after completing his Matric in 2008. The Art rich Westville campus enviroment allowed him to explose other art forms. He joined Ncooh Entertainment mid-semester where he focused mainly on poetry, theatre and eventually heading the club. After a couple of years he became a member of Keen Artist Theatre. He went on to become Poetry co-ordinator, Hip/Hop co-ordinator and eventually Director of Keen Artist Theatre. During his time as KAT director he met This Guy That Guy, Elohymn, Zolani, Yongama and Mary-Street later on and the Kids where born... Ask your GrandMa about it.
